Carrom is a popular board game played in India, which involves flicking small disks into pockets using a striker. The game is usually played with two or four players, and the goal is to score points by pocketing your own disks while preventing your opponent from pocketing theirs. To set up the game, a square board with four pockets in the corners is placed on a table or other flat surface. Each player takes turns using a striker to flick their disks into the pockets. The player with the most points at the end of the game wins.
